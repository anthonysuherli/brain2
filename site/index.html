◌ the problem
9.5min

The visible cost — the time to rebuild mental context after one interruption. The deeper cost is quieter: every insight you don't write down evaporates, and nothing you learn in one repo ever carries to the next.

# knowledge that doesn't compound
6 switches × 9.5 min = ≈57 min/day

Nearly an hour a day reconstructing state you already had — and a graph of hard-won context that resets to zero with every new repo. brain2 keeps it instead, so the next session starts ahead.

◌ how it works

It maintains itself. You guide it once — at the schema.

01 background

Watch

A subagent watches you work and snapshots state on every change — branch, files, diff, a one-line hypothesis — then distills each session into concepts in your graph. No command to run, no note to write.

02 guided

Organize

brain2 tracks what doesn't fit its ontology. When that residual crosses a threshold — or you've collected enough to start — it offers, at a turn boundary, to design the schema with you: one question at a time, you steer, it applies.

03 portable

Ground

Anywhere you work, tap the graph for ~1,750 tokens of concepts over evidence — scoped to where you are, drawn from every repo. On the cloud tier the same slice follows you across machines.

Everything but the schema step runs in the background and fails silent. See the knowledge engine underneath ›
