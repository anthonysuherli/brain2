Executive summary

br8n captures workspace state the moment a developer is interrupted — branch, open files, diff, and a one-line "why" — and replays it as a 30-second resume card. It ships freemium: a fully-local free tier (SQLite, loopback, no auth) and a paid cloud tier (Supabase) for sync & cross-repo search.

SITUATION

v0 has shipped: two-tier storage behind a unified Store protocol, distributed as a Claude Code plugin + VS Code extension. The product works; the question is how to take it to market as a solo founder.

COMPLICATION

Developers reject hype and buy on hands-on evidence. A paid cloud tier that stores code-adjacent data adds real compliance, trust, and conversion hurdles — amplified by a one-person team.

RESOLUTION

Lead with a verifiable "your code never leaves your machine" free tier, distribute through peer-led channels, offload tax to a Merchant-of-Record, and instrument a clear activation funnel before spending a dollar on acquisition.

Narrow wedge on the interrupt→resume moment. Launch to solo AI-devs + agentic power users; defer the multi-repo senior.

HN spike, marketplaces compound

WS-3 · PRICING

Freemium, storage = the gate

Free local forever · Pro $8/mo (sync + cross-repo) · Team $12/seat. Never gate the 30-sec aha.

WS-4 · PAYMENTS

Merchant-of-Record, not raw Stripe

Lemon Squeezy offloads global VAT/sales-tax liability; dunning recovers 30–60% of failed payments.

WS-5 · LEGAL & TRUST

Highest risk — it stores code

Single-member LLC + ToS/PP/DPA before charging. SOC 2 only on first enterprise deal. No-train/no-retain is the key disclosure.

WS-6 · METRICS

North Star: weekly restoring resumes

Activation = first capture→restoring resume. Content-free PostHog events. Hard go/no-go gates.

WS-8 · ONBOARDING

Aha in session one

Install → auto-capture on interrupt → first populated resume card. Shareable card is the growth loop.

Payments & billing

Launch on a Merchant-of-Record (Lemon Squeezy), not raw Stripe. The ~1.5–2pt premium buys away global VAT/GST/sales-tax registration, filing, remittance, and liability — the decisive factor for a solo founder.

08

Legal, privacy & trust

The highest-risk workstream, because br8n stores code-adjacent data. The strategy pre-SOC 2 is architecture + transparency, not certifications.

Minimum kit before charging

  • Single-member LLC + EIN + business bank account
  • Merchant-of-Record for tax offload (Lemon Squeezy/Paddle)
  • ToS + Privacy Policy + DPA (Termly/GetTerms ~$10–49 → one lawyer pass)
  • /security page + public sub-processor list
  • Confirm LLM/embedding no-train/no-retain terms — the #1 disclosure
  • Self-serve data export + delete; egress disclosed at the enable seam

Trust lead: "your code never leaves your machine" is literally true on the free tier (loopback, no network) — a claim most AI tools can't make. Verifiable beats marketed.

SOC 2 — defer until the first enterprise deal

Type I · 3–6 mo
$25k–$40k
Type II · 6–12 mo
$45k–$70k

Trigger = first serious deal that gates on it. Start Type I for fast credibility (40–60% of cost credits toward Type II within 12 mo), then Type II for sustained enterprise demand. Use Vanta/Drata/Comp AI. Pre-write SIG/CAIQ answers now — the architecture already satisfies most.

Why existential: a missing LLC, an unconfirmed no-train term, or a privacy claim the architecture doesn't back is a trust-and-liability event no amount of HN traction survives. Treat WS-5 sign-off as a hard precondition of charging money.

09

Critical path & the one big risk

The smallest set of must-dos

  1. W0 — the live capture→resume loop works on both tiers, instrumented. Everything measurable hangs off it.
  2. The aha is reachable in session 1 — guaranteed first capture + a populated card, no gap dead-end.
  3. One demo GIF + a validated wedge. The GIF powers every channel; the wedge must paraphrase clean (≥5/10).
  4. Show HN, executed with discipline — problem-framed title, founder first comment, 6h thread camp.
  5. Pass the go/no-go — activation ≥25% AND crash-free ≥99.5% before posting.

If selling at launch, add (6) the paid surface live and legal. If not ready — launch free-only and defer the paid gate; the free spike doesn't depend on it.

⚠ Biggest risk: WS-5 (legal/privacy/trust)

Every other workstream's downside is a slow launch. WS-5's downside is existential — br8n captures branches, file paths, diffs, and intent, and on the cloud tier that egresses to Supabase + LLM APIs.

Mitigations, on the critical path:

  • Make the verifiable "code never leaves your machine" free tier the trust lead.
  • Confirm + document LLM no-train/no-retain terms before W4 close.
  • One fixed-fee lawyer pass on ToS/Privacy/DPA before the first paid dollar.
  • Publish /security with the live sub-processor list before cloud is sellable.
